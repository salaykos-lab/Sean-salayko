Professional Introduction

My name is Sean Salayko, and I am a Packaging Engineering Intern at Milwaukee Tool, working on the Power Tools Accessories team. Throughout my internship, I supported a wide range of packaging projects with a focus on design execution, cross-functional collaboration, and data-driven analysis.

In this role, I contributed to packaging design efforts, assisted with testing and documentation, and supported various project-related data mining and analysis tasks used to inform packaging decisions. I regularly collaborated with other packaging engineers, helping support active projects while also taking opportunities to lead select tasks and projects when appropriate.

Project 1

Interactive Bit Holder

Developed an interactive clamshell packaging concept that incorporated a hands-on feature to enhance product engagement and presentation. The goal was to translate a conceptual idea into a physical prototype using both CAD software and in-house manufacturing tools.

  • Designed the 3D clamshell package and interactive insert using SolidWorks and ArtiosCAD
  • Created the insert card geometry to integrate seamlessly within the clamshell
  • Prepared CAD files for physical production and prototyping
  • Manufactured the prototype by cutting components on the Zünd and thermoforming the clamshell
  • Collaborated with the project team to review fit, functionality, and overall concept execution

Project 2

Trimmer Line

The objective of this project was to identify a more cost-effective and sustainable packaging solution for Milwaukee Tool's trimmer line assortment while clearly differentiating new SKUs from existing products and maintaining strong visual appeal.

  • Developed and evaluated multiple packaging concepts focused on cost reduction and sustainability improvements
  • Explored alternative materials, structures, and formats to meet sustainability requirements while maintaining performance
  • Considered visual differentiation and shelf appeal with Product Marketing
  • Created physical prototypes to compare concepts and communicate design intent
  • Compiled a prototype comparison document used to request supplier quotes
Concept Development Physical Prototyping Cost Evaluation Sustainability Supplier Quotes
Project 3

15 PC Bit Set

Developed a new packaging solution for a 15-piece bit set using an existing footprint while improving product presentation and aligning with Brand Marketing expectations. Multiple packaging structures were evaluated to find the best balance of function, presentation, and manufacturability.

  • Evaluated trap blister, single-face blister, and clamshell packaging formats
  • Created packaging concepts for all three configurations
  • Led concept development that resulted in selecting the trap blister as the preferred solution
  • Built a physical prototype of the trap blister package for Brand Marketing
  • Finalized the dieline and sent it to PRC to initiate sample development
Packaging Concepts Blister Format Evaluation Prototype Development Dielines
Project 4

Foam Insert

Designed and produced a custom foam insert for a Packout case to safely secure tools for shipment to a different state. The project required rapid turnaround while ensuring proper fit, protection, and readiness for shipment.

  • Worked with the Concepting team to align on tool layout, protection needs, and Packout constraints
  • Retrieved necessary tool and case files and converted them into 2D CAD files for foam layout
  • Designed the foam cut geometry to accurately fit each tool and prevent movement during transit
  • Cut and fabricated all foam inserts within a three-day timeline
  • Installed the foam inserts into the Packout and verified shipment readiness

Workplace Experience

My day-to-day work during my internship varied a lot, which made the experience really unique. Some days were meetings-heavy, where I was working with my team and cross-functional groups to align on projects. Other days I spent most of my time in the lab concepting, designing, and building prototypes. There were also days focused on project documentation, organizing information, and reviewing data to support ongoing packaging work.

I had a good balance between working independently and collaborating with my team. Having both allowed me to take ownership of tasks while still learning from others and getting feedback along the way. Because priorities could change day-to-day, the environment was fast-paced and required me to stay flexible and organized.

What made this experience stand out was that no two days were ever the same. The variety of work kept things interesting and gave me exposure to many parts of the packaging development process, helping me better understand how everything comes together in a real engineering environment.

Industry Insights & Reflection

Throughout my time at Milwaukee Tool, my view of the packaging industry changed significantly. I learned that packaging engineers take full ownership of their projects and responsibilities, from early concepts all the way through execution. The role requires balancing multiple priorities at once, while still being accountable for decisions and outcomes.

The internship also showed me how fast-paced the packaging environment can be. Each project moved quickly, often requiring flexibility and strong communication to keep things on track. Through this experience, I grew both technically and professionally.

On the technical side, I gained experience using software tools such as ArtiosCAD, NX, and SolidWorks, as well as hands-on exposure to lab equipment including the Zünd cutter and various testing machines. Professionally, I developed important skills such as organization, time management, prioritization, and clear communication.

Overall, this internship helped confirm my interest in packaging engineering and showed me what it looks like to work in a real, fast-moving product development environment.
